## The problem

**The energy sector runs on a very specific rhythm: contracts with end dates, renewals to anticipate, clients that need follow-up.** Managing that in Excel doesn't scale: it doesn't remind you, it doesn't prioritise, it doesn't remember who spoke to whom or when.

The team was losing renewals through lack of visibility. There was no quick way to know which contracts were expiring that month, which incidents were still open, or which rep was responsible for each client. The information existed — it was just scattered everywhere.

---

## The solution

We built a management dashboard that gives the team what they need at a glance: how many active clients there are, which contracts are about to expire, how many incidents are still open and who needs to act.

<Image src={dashboardMd} alt="Responsive dashboard on medium screen" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />

Each client profile groups everything in one place: contact details, energy contracts with their supply points, validity dates, amounts and commissions, attached documents, active incidents and a full history of every action logged on that account.

<Image src={clientDetails} alt="Full client profile with contracts, documents and activity history" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />

### Renewals with a priority queue

The renewals module was the most critical for the client. It automatically shows all contracts expiring in the next 60 days, sorted by urgency — overdue, urgent (under 30 days) and upcoming — with the last contact logged and the channel used (phone or email).

<Image src={renovations} alt="Renewal queue with urgency, days remaining and last contact" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />

From that same screen, the rep can log a contact, note the outcome and renew the contract directly — without leaving the flow.

<Image src={registerCall} alt="Renewal contact registration dialog" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />

### Search and filters that work

Client search is real-time — by name, ID number or company — with combinable filters by status, assigned rep and registration period. The list can be exported to CSV in one click, filtered exactly as shown on screen.

<Image src={searchMd} alt="Client list with active search and filters" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />

### Incidents with priority levels

Incidents (pending invoices, missing ID documents, unsigned contracts) are managed in a separate table with four priority levels. Any team member can open, update and close them. The dashboard always reflects them in real time.

<Image src={incidentsTable} alt="Incident table with priority and status per client" class="rounded-2xl my-6" />
