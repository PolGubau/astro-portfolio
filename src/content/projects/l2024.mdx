## The challenge

Every last Saturday of 2024, <At label='Marcos Rayo' href='https://www.linkedin.com/in/marcos-rayo-garc%C3%ADa-308342308/'/> and I walked one full metro line of Barcelona - above ground, on foot, stop to stop. No shortcuts, no metro rides. Just the streets.

By the end of the year we had covered all 12 lines of the network: ~200 km of city at walking pace.

<Image
  alt="Group of people walking one of the metro lines"
  width={800}
  height={500}
  className="rounded-2xl w-full object-cover show-animation my-2"
  src={people}
/>

Friends joined on different dates - some for one line, some for several. Each Saturday ended up being its own thing: different neighbourhoods, different weather, different conversations.

## The website

The goal was to have somewhere to document the whole thing - routes, stats, and the photos we took at each stop.

<Image
  alt="Map overview showing all walked metro lines across Barcelona"
  width={800}
  height={500}
  className="rounded-2xl w-full object-cover show-animation my-2"
  src={mapOverview}
/>

Built with **React**, **TypeScript**, and **MapLibre** - no Google Maps, no API keys. Each line's GPS route was exported from our phones, cleaned up in [GPX Studio](https://gpx.studio), converted to GeoJSON, and loaded as a map layer. Selecting a line highlights it on the map and shows the route stats.

<Image
  alt="Map zoomed in showing a single line with stops and route detail"
  width={800}
  height={500}
  className="rounded-2xl w-full object-cover show-animation my-2"
  src={mapDetail}
/>

Each line includes distance walked, time on foot, average pace, number of stations, and the date we did it. All pulled from the GPS file metadata.

<Image
  alt="Line detail panel showing stats like distance, time and stations"

  height={500}

  src={lineDetails}
/>

## Stop photos

At every station we took a photo. Clicking a stop on the map opens the photo taken there - the images live in a public folder, named by line and stop, loaded dynamically on click.
