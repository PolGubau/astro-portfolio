Every summer, Mataró celebrates **Les Santes** — six days of concerts, traditional parades, fire runs, and open-air events across the whole city. Hundreds of things happen simultaneously, spread across dozens of locations.

The problem? The only official programme was a printed PDF.

So I built the app myself.

<Image src={landingThumbnail} alt="Les Santes landing page" class="show-animation rounded-2xl my-6" />

---

## Why this project exists

I live near Mataró and go to Les Santes every year. Each time I found myself — and everyone around me — pulling out their phone to Google *"what time is the correfoc"* or *"where are the Gegants right now"*. Nobody had a clear answer.

The festival deserves better tooling. So I reached out to the city council, built the app, and published everything as **open source under MIT**.

> A solo, public-good project. No client, no budget, no team. Just a problem worth solving.

---

## The app

The mobile app (React Native + Expo) is designed around one central question: **what's happening right now?**

### 🕐 Ara (Now)

The home tab shows a live card with the current event and a strip of upcoming ones. No configuration needed — open the app, know what's on.

### 🗺️ Mapa (Map)

An interactive map powered by MapLibre. Static events appear as pins; moving parades (cercaviles, gegants) show animated route polylines with their real-time estimated position — calculated by interpolating elapsed time along the planned route.

<Image src={appMapa} alt="App map with event routes" class="show-animation rounded-2xl my-6" />

### 📅 Agenda

The full programme, filterable by day, category (family / night / traditional / cultural), type of event, or proximity. Plus a favourites system so you never miss what you care about.

## Key design choices

These are the decisions that made the biggest difference — and the ones I'm most proud of.

### No login. No accounts. Ever.

Festival attendees don't want to register to check what time a parade starts. The app is 100% anonymous — just open it and go. This also simplified App Store and Play Store approval dramatically.

### Offline-first

Festival venues have terrible mobile signal. The app caches all event data locally with a timestamp, so it keeps working even without internet. A banner shows the cache age when offline.

### Catalan as the primary language

Les Santes is a Catalan cultural festival. The app speaks Catalan first, with English as fallback. Not an afterthought — it was the first decision made.

### AVIF everywhere

The full poster archive (from 1892 to today) would be impractical at original quality. Converting to AVIF brought a ~97% size reduction — from 5.2 MB of PNGs down to ~146 KB for all app screenshots.

### MapLibre in a WebView

Instead of pulling in Google Maps or Apple Maps native SDKs (with their privacy implications and setup overhead), the map runs as a MapLibre GL JS instance inside a WebView. Custom MapTiler tiles, full control, no tracking.

## The admin panel

A password-protected back-office embedded in the landing. The festival organiser can create, edit, and cancel events — with real-time announcements pushed to all app users.

<Image src={adminHome} alt="Admin panel - event list" class="show-animation rounded-2xl my-6" />

Event creation handles both fixed-location events and moving parades (uploaded as GeoJSON routes from geojson.io). Cancellations require a reason, shown to users inside the app.

<Image src={adminEditEvent} alt="Admin panel - edit event form" class="show-animation rounded-2xl my-6" />

## Tech at a glance

| Layer | Stack |
|---|---|
| **Mobile app** | React Native, Expo SDK 54, Expo Router |

| **Map** | MapLibre GL JS in a WebView, MapTiler tiles |
